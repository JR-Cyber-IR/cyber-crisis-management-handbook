Cyber crises are managed under pressure, uncertainty, and incomplete information.
These principles help organizations remain effective despite those constraints.

---

## ðŸ§© Fundamental Principles

### 1. Clarity of Authority
Decisions must have a **clear owner**.
Ambiguity in authority delays action.

---

### 2. Timely Decisions Over Perfect Information
Waiting for full certainty increases damage.
Decisions should be **reversible when possible**, but timely.

---

### 3. One Crisis, One Voice
Internal and external communication must be **consistent and coordinated**.

---

### 4. Business First, Security Informed
Technical input supports decisions, but **business impact drives priorities**.

---

### 5. Structured Coordination
Clear roles, rituals, and reporting prevent chaos.

---

### 6. Documentation in Real Time
Decisions, actions, and rationale must be logged for:
- Legal protection
- Post-crisis review
- Organizational learning

> A cyber crisis is not solved by technology alone,
