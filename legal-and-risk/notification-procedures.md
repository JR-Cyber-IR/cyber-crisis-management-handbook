Step-by-step procedures for notifying regulatory authorities, customers, and stakeholders during cybersecurity incidents.

## ‚è≥ Pre-Incident Preparation

Maintain an updated list of regulatory contacts:

- **Data Protection Authority (DPA)** - National supervisory authority
- **National Cybersecurity Authority** - Security regulator
- **Industry-Specific Regulators** - Sector-specific contacts
- **Customer Escalation Contacts** - Customer notification channels
- **Legal Counsel** - Internal and external legal contacts
- **PR/Communications** - Media relations contacts

Develop and pre-approve notification templates that comply with regulatory requirements in multiple languages.

## üïú Incident Assessment Phase

**Step 1: Determine Affected Data Types** (1 hour)
- Personal data (names, SSN, contact info)
- Financial data (credit cards, bank accounts)
- Health data (medical records)
- Proprietary data (intellectual property)
- Authentication credentials

**Step 2: Risk Assessment** (4 hours)
- **High Risk**: Direct personal data exposure, ransomware with exfiltration
- **Medium Risk**: Encrypted data exposed, keys compromised
- **Low Risk**: Data accessed but not exfiltrated

**Step 3: Determine Applicable Regulations** (2 hours)
Consult the Regulatory Landscape document:
- EU Regulations (RGPD, NIS2, DORA)
- US Regulations (SEC, State Laws, HIPAA)
- International Regulations (PIPEDA, Privacy Act)

**Step 4: Identify Regulatory Bodies** (4 hours)
- Primary supervisor/competent authority
- Multiple regulators (if multi-jurisdiction)
- Secondary regulatory bodies

## üìÑ Notification Execution

### Authority Notification

**Required Information**:
- Date and time of discovery
- Nature and scope of breach
- Number of data subjects affected
- Categories of personal data involved
- Likely consequences for data subjects
- Measures taken to address breach
- Contact point for further information

**Channels**: Email (official addresses), Web portals, Phone (critical incidents)

### Customer Notification

**Trigger**: Reasonably likely risk of harm to customers

**Content**: Clear language, what data affected, what happened, measures taken, support resources

**Methods**: Email (primary), Website notices, Media announcements, Direct mail

### RGPD Timeline
| Action | Deadline | Responsible |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Initial Assessment | 4 hours | CISO/Legal |

| Action | Deadline | Responsible |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Preliminary Alert | 24 hours | CISO |
| Full Report | 72 hours | CISO/Legal |
| Final Report | 30 days | CISO/Legal |

### SEC Timeline (Public Companies)
| Action | Deadline | Responsible |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Assess Materiality | 4 business days | Legal/CFO |
| File Form 8-K | 4 business days | IR/Legal |
| Public Disclosure | 4 business days | PR/CEO |

## üñ≠Ô∏è Multi-Jurisdiction Approach

**Phase 1 (24 hours)**:
- Notify primary regulatory authorities
- Issue preliminary customer notification
- Activate incident response protocol

**Phase 2 (72 hours)**:
- Notify secondary regulatory authorities
- Issue comprehensive customer notification
- Prepare public statement

**Phase 3 (30 days)**:
- Submit final investigation reports
- Complete regulatory follow-up
- Implement corrective measures

## üìÅ Documentation

Maintain a **Breach Registry** documenting:
- Incident and discovery dates
- Data types and volumes affected
- Notification dates and methods
- Regulatory bodies notified
- Regulatory responses
- Corrective measures

## üîÑ Post-Notification Actions

1. **Monitor Regulatory Response** - Track communications, respond within deadlines
2. **Customer Support** - Establish support hotline, provide resources
3. **Incident Resolution** - Document remediation, schedule follow-up notifications
